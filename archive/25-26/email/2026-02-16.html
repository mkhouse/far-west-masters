Board elections are coming up! All current officers are willing to continue serving, but several Vice Chair positions are open for nomination if you’re interested in getting involved. We Need a Membership Director!

After 10 years of dedicated service to Far West Masters, Mary Feddis is stepping down as Vice Chair of Membership. Thank you, Mary, for everything you’ve given to FWM!

The Membership Director is the link between our racers and our race operations—the go-to person for answering questions, helping new racers get started, managing bibs, verifying memberships, and coordinating with our scoring director and race administrators. We’re looking for someone with strong communication skills, comfort with data systems, and a customer service mindset.

Interested? Know someone who’d be great? Reach out to Mary at membership@farwestmasters.org to learn more, or read the full board elections article for details on the Membership Director and all open positions.

2026 Masters Nationals at Mammoth Mountain

The 2026 U.S. Ski & Snowboard Alpine Masters National Championships are coming to Mammoth Mountain, March 22–28, right here in the Far West division. This is a full week of racing across all four disciplines: Downhill, Super G, Giant Slalom, and Slalom, plus social events and the closing banquet on Saturday evening.

The late fee deadline is Sunday, March 8. Registrations after that date are subject to a $20 late fee per race. Discounted competitor lift tickets are available, and slope-side lodging packages at the Mammoth Mountain Inn can be booked online or by calling 800-MAMMOTH and referencing USSA/FIS Masters Nationals 2026 (Booking ID: 37013). The lodging block cutoff is also March 8.

For the full event schedule and details, visit the U.S. Ski & Snowboard Masters events page.

Opt In for Text Messages

We text important race announcements, like schedule changes and cancellations, only to racers who have opted in and are signed up for that race weekend. With weather cancellations this season, text updates have been especially helpful for keeping racers informed in real time.
